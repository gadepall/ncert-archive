Let $\vec{O} = \myvec{0\\0}$ be the intersection point of the diagonals of the rhombus. 
The diagonals of a rhombus bisect one another at right angles. Hence, 
\begin{align}
    \vec{B} = \myvec{2.8\\0}\\
    \vec{E} = \myvec{0\\-3.25}\\
    \vec{N} = \myvec{-2.8\\0}\\
    \vec{D} = \myvec{0\\3.25}
\end{align}
